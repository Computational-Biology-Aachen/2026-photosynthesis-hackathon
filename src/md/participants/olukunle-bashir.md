# Summary
Olukunle Babatunde Bashir is a doctoral student of Federal University of Agriculture, Abeokuta and a research fellow with the Cassava Breeding Unit of the International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (IITA), Nigeria.  A plant breeder and a data enthusiast, presently, is working on the Photosynthesis Efficiency of 25 Provitamin A Cassava Genotypes for Assimilate Partitioning and Root Yield.  His research focus on using high throughput device for evaluation of phenotypic traits and understanding the nexus between photosynthetic and physiological traits. 

In this hackhaton, he would explore on how photosynthesis can be used for food security in sub-Saharan Africa and get a deep insight from experts on the role photosynthesis plays in the functioning and development of crop.
