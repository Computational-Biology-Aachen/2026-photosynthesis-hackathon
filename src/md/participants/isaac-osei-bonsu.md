# Summary

Isaac Osei-Bonsu is a Senior Research Scientist specializing in Crop Physiology and Agronomy at the Crops Research Institute (CRI) of the Council for Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR), based in Kumasi, Ghana. He is also an adjunct lecturer of Crop Physiology at the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST), teaching undergraduate and graduate-level Plant Physiology courses at the Department of Crop and Soil Sciences. He had his PhD from Michigan State University where he worked on understanding photosynthesis responses to thermal stress in cowpea under Prof David Kramer. He has extensive experience in photosynthetic research, particularly on cowpea where his work has focused on gas exchange, and chlorophyll fluorescence measurements, and understanding plant responses to environmental stress, which are areas that have shaped his interest in improving crop productivity.
He is motivated to participate in the photosynthesis hackathon because it provides a unique interdisciplinary platform where physiology, computational approaches, data science and engineering can intersect to proffer solutions that cannot be achieved by individual disciplines. He is joining this hackathon to engage with innovative ideas, collaborate with diverse experts, and contribute to solutions that can advance photosynthesis research and its applications in crop improvement.
