# Summary

Elouen Corvest completed his Bachelor's and Master's Theses at the Matuszyńska lab at RWTH Aachen. There, he gained deep insight into computational modelling and deepened his knowledge of photosynthesis by examining and testing many already published models. Over the years, he has found the issues, advantages, and uses of computational modelling in photosynthesis research. So much so, in fact, that he has built the GreenSloth project from the ground up.

GreenSloth is an ecosystem that aims to address many of the issues Elouen has found in his research on models by greatly limiting the scope of the model categories. This allows the solution to be tailored to these types of models and enables new ways to compare models, making it easier for someone to decide which model is best for their use. While GreenSloth is still in its infancy, Elouen has great hope that it will reach the right audience and become an integral part of the scientific community.
