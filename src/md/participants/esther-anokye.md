# Summary

Esther Anokye is a research scientist (plant physiologist) at the Cocoa Research Institute of Ghana (CRIG). She is interested in understanding source-sink relationships and how environmental stresses impact cocoa, coffee, cashew, kola, and shea. She combines gauze-house and field experiments to explore genetic variation in growth and developmental dynamics, physiology, and metabolism under favourable and abiotic stress conditions. Deepening her expertise in productive photosynthesis, advanced plant behaviour, and plant-
environment interactions is essential for selecting climate-smart crop varieties. This is particularly urgent for perennial crops like cocoa, which support smallholder livelihoods in West Africa. In the coming decades, a major challenge for cocoa production will be selecting varieties that can withstand decreased rainfall, rising temperatures, and soil degradation driven by climate change. The program’s focus aligns closely with her research goals and has strengthened her belief that advancing her knowledge of plant physiological mechanisms will enhance both her scientific
contributions and institutional impact. Strengthening these competencies will enable her to collaborate more effectively with interdisciplinary teams, contribute to resilient crop improvement strategies, and mentor the next generation of agricultural scientists committed to sustainable production systems.
